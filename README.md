# Chronic Disease Burden Among Young Adults in G20 Nations

## Overview

This project is a Power BI dashboard analyzing the impact of chronic diseases among young adults (ages 19–35) across G20 countries. The goal is to explore patterns in mortality, disease prevalence, healthcare access, and treatment costs to better understand factors affecting health outcomes.

## Dataset

The dataset was obtained from Kaggle and includes health and socioeconomic indicators such as mortality rate, prevalence rate, recovery rate, healthcare access, treatment cost, DALYs, income, education, and healthcare infrastructure.

## Tools Used

* Microsoft Power BI
* Power Query
* DAX
* Kaggle dataset

## Visualizations

The dashboard includes:

* KPI cards showing average mortality, DALYs, recovery rate, and healthcare access
* Scatter plot comparing healthcare access and mortality rate by country
* Donut chart showing disease burden by gender
* Line chart showing prevalence trends by disease over time
* Pie chart comparing treatment costs across G20 countries
* Key Influencers visual identifying factors driving higher mortality rates

## Key Findings

The analysis shows that countries with higher healthcare access tend to have lower mortality rates among young adults. Socioeconomic factors such as income and education also influence health outcomes, while treatment costs vary significantly across G20 nations.
